How to Build High-Quality OPR Lists
Game Mode and Mobility

Base OPR or tournament pack have different win conditions with varying objectives and deployment rules.
Mobility is a core necessity for all lists, especially for event packs with progressive scoring and unusual deployment zones.

    Scout units and transports maybe more than ambush ?

Activation Count and Mobility Impact

    7 activations at 2,000 points as a starting point.

More activations = better tactical flexibility, objective control, and the ability to delay decisions during the game.

Mobility and activation count are closely linked : they are game options.

Big tough units and transports can help but must be supported by sufficient activations and mobility.
Unit Variety, Activation Priority, and Decision-Making

    More activations and a variety of units = better options on the board
    Activation priority is key to maximize impact. (think ambush)
    Too many high-cost units : one will activate late and have reduced value.

Reinforces the idea of throwaway activations (cap/contest VS a stronger unit)
Competitive vs. Thematic Lists

    Competitive : win ! Then look for mobility, activation count, AP (AP4) and deadly.
    Thematic lists : identify weaknesses and mitigate them to remain viable.

Mini agnostic so use cool minis to keep the theme but improve the list viability.
Unit Evaluation and Army Book Analysis

    Look for AP sources (AP4 idealy) to deal with tough units.
    Mobility keywords (Scout, Fast, Strider, Flying).
    Morale bonuses (Fearless) to be activable and contest
    Damage keywords : Furious, Shred, and Surge
    Quality & number of attacks are linked
    Defense
    Hero auras and spell casting : improve lethality or mobility

Unit Structure and Damage Analysis

Unit constructions in OPR = blocks of 3, 5, 10, or 20 "wounds".
These are thresholds for damage to kill units or at least force morale.

    Target six wounds for an effective damage output activation.
    (2T3, T12 to half, 10T1 to half)

Overkilling is inefficient, like underperforming.
Filling Army Roles and Covering Weaknesses

    Pick primary lethal units (high AP high attacks)
    Identify and fill gaps : chaff counters, survivability, and morale support.
    Redundancy : 2 copies of good units to cover multiple board areas.
    Lacking AP or deadly ? Look for spells, debuffs, or other utility units to compensate.

Identify and react to a weakness can be :

    a transport for glass cannons OR more activations (so the opponent can't react efficiently)
    ignore but then play around

Iterative List Building and Meta

Test lists, refine through play (TTS to play more ?), adapt to the local meta.

Aim for balanced generalist lists unless committed to skew or theme.

Have fun > win obviously.
Q&A
Competitive vs. Flavor Lists

Competitive lists focus on AP, mobility, activations, and deadly units, while flavor lists prioritize theme and fun but must cover critical weaknesses.
Activation Counts

Minimum viable activations at 2,000 points is about five, with seven being ideal. Higher activation counts offer more tactical flexibility.
Identifying Good Units

Look for hyper-specialized units excelling at a role, with high quality, AP, and keywords like Fast, Furious, or Shred.
Trying New Armies Without Minis

Use Tabletop Simulator or proxy minis to test before committing to paint and purchase.
Ratios of Unit Types

Unless skew, balance :

    Melee (shake on charge, cheaper) & ranged (threat zone)
    Consider generalists & units that are objective players
    1 to 3 scouts to counter (meta dependent too ?)
    Ambush : target first activation on Turn2, so 4 activation on the board Turn1 ?
    Deadly and blast : one of each. Mind game because of the threat it poses.

Odd numbers for ambush & scout is ideal.
List starting point

H : Strong/good/cool unit : it's the core, and the rest supports it.
D : T9 or T12 or aura (damage = toughness), scout, specialists (deadly, blast), gaps
Heroes in Units

    Aura to multiply damage
    Before doubling a unit (to reach damage threshold) if cheaper
    hide the hero

expensive hero is unlikely to join
Doubling Up Units

    A2T1 : double up
    Double units core to strategy (and cost-effective)
    Avoid doubling up low-value or fragile units : overkill is lost damage

Point Level Adjustments

Max activations at lower points
At higher point values, expect bigger units, more AP, and more deadly units.
Lower points means chaff so blast.
Personnal pref : 2000 GDF & 1500 AoF (constraints are fun)
Casting Strategy

Bring multiple casters if the spell list contains good damage, to make it more reliable.

Bring the "right amount" if you need to cast THE utility spell.
Good if that's only one (cheap) caster to apply the buff !

Spells+fight means you can target multiple units.
Build for Age of Fantasy VS Grimdark Future

GDF AP is king, mobility is more important in AoF (because AP is less prevalent).
For AoF, keep an eye on impact (quality doesn't matter), banner (improve morale is always good) and spells.
Threat assessment and activation priority when list building

Points as a starting heuristic.
Ignore or focus the big points units in you opponent's army.
Threat assessment :

    unit that threatens your strategy
    big unit you can kill

Can't kill the unit ? ignore it / play around it.
Building for Missions

Tailor lists for the most challenging missions in tournament packs.
Usually focusing on mobility => scout units currently.
Consider side missions if able, don't prioritize it over everything.
Cookie cutter roles ?

Core & Throwaway activations.
Include cheap units (swarms, naked heroes) to fill activation gaps and contest objectives without risking valuable units.
When to Skew or Generalize

Use generalist lists for competitive play.
Skew lists are high risk/reward and best for casual or narrative games.
Max Points on Single Unit

Usually avoid spending more than 25% of points on one unit unless it is durable and core to the strategy.
Make sure to have cheap units to compensate activations.
Progressive vs. Basic Objective Scoring

Progressive scoring demands aggressive early objective control, high mobility, and many activations. Scout and transports are great, ambush is bad.
Basic scoring allows slower, more varied strategies including ambush and shooting. Scout is still great.
When is it worth burying a hero in chaff?

To protect a valuable caster and some buffers (buffs/marks, quality for morale).
Do you aim to wipe out armor by a certain turn or enough to wipe out by the end of the game?

    Aim : wipe in one activation.
    Need more that 2 activations to kill ? Ignore.

T12 is still as potent if it lost tough points. Hard to be a valuable target.
