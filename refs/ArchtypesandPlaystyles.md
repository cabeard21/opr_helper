How Each One Page Rules Army Wins Games: Archtypes and Playstyles Breakdown

Discussion about :

    Archetypes – what armies naturally tend to do.
    Play styles – how lists actually try to win on the board.

The goal is to help players:

    Understand what to expect from a faction.
    Analyze an opponent’s list more effectively.
    Build more intentional, cohesive lists.

Archetypes

An archetype is what you roughly expect when someone says what army they are playing.

Archetypes are presented on a grid with two axes :

    Defense – Balance – Offense
    Horde – Rounded – Elite

Offensive Archetype

An offensive army:

    Spends very little on defensive abilities.
    Maximizes damage output per wound.
    Tries to out-trade the opponent.
    Often relies on wiping units in a single activation.
    Is usually fragile.

OPR is very lethal, which makes aggressive playstyles effective.

Core concepts :

    Aggro ambush
    One tapping units
    Out-mobilizing or out-DPSing the opponent

    Offense does not mean zero defense ! The attack investment heavily outweighs the defense investment.

Defensive Archetype

A defensive army:

    Is not interested in the offensive arms race.
    Focuses on durability and staying power.
    Withstands the initial charge.
    Wins through counter-strike and resilience.
    Often has strong morale.

Defensive lists are less common in OPR, especially in Grim Dark Future.

    Defense can invalidate mobility and aggression investments by simply absorbing them.

Elite Archetype

An elite army:

    Brings fewer, more expensive models.
    Has higher quality and more reliable output.
    Often includes Fearless or high Tough values.
    Controls objectives with minimal bodies.
    Maintains effectiveness until its final wound.

Elite does not automatically mean defensive.
An aggressive elite list is high risk, high reward.
Horde Archetype

A horde army:

    Maximizes total wounds on the board.
    Has high activation count.
    Overwhelms through sheer volume.
    Forces the opponent to deal with too many bodies.

    Can the elite armies remove enough wounds over four rounds ?

Play Styles

Play styles describe how an army plans to win.
Positional Counterplay

Uses:

    Ambush
    Scout
    Aircraft
    High mobility

Goal: outmaneuver the opponent and win through positioning.
Flank Overload

Focuses heavy resources on one flank.

Win one side decisively, draw or stall the other.
Shove It In

Highly aggressive early board pressure.

Push forward immediately and restrict opponent movement.
Slow Roll

Gradual advance.

Whittle the opponent down before committing.

Often plays for round four objective captures.
Round 2 Go

Frontloads power into round two.

Often caster heavy and ambush heavy.

Explosive turn with maximum token expenditure.

Works well with extreme spell stacking via Spell Accumulator stuff.
Control

Uses:

    Mind control
    Difficult terrain
    Redeployment
    Movement manipulation

Goal: disrupt opponent plans rather than overpower directly.
Conclusion

    Archetypes help predict tendencies.
    Play styles help build intentionally.
    Armies are more than their armywide rule.
    Version 3.5 changes create new ways to play existing factions.

Tips for list building :

    Start with a play style in mind.
    Build toward it deliberately.
    Experiment with different approaches inside the same book.

    The game supports far more flexibility than it appears at first glance.
