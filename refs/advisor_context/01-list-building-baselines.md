# List Building Baselines

- Build toward a clear plan instead of only picking high-efficiency units.
- At 2,000 points, use about 7 activations as a healthy default. At smaller games, keep at least 5 activations when the book allows it.
- Activations are tactical options: they let the army delay key commitments, contest objectives, and avoid wasting expensive units on low-value moves.
- Mobility is mandatory in most lists. Value Scout, Fast, Flying, Strider, transports, and other ways to reach or contest objectives.
- Include cheap or disposable units when needed to hold, screen, contest, or preserve activation count without exposing the army's main threats.
- Avoid spending more than about 25% of the list on one unit unless it is durable, central to the strategy, and supported by enough cheaper activations.
- Balance the list across core damage, objective play, ranged pressure, melee threat, morale support, and a few throwaway activations.
- Prefer redundancy for key jobs: two credible tools in different board areas are usually safer than one isolated solution.
