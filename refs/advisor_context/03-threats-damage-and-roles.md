# Threats, Damage, and Roles

- Evaluate units by job, not only by points efficiency. A good list needs objective pieces, damage pieces, screens, support, and answers to skew.
- For killing plans, aim for units or combined activations that can pressure roughly 6 wounds. This often forces morale or removes common targets.
- Avoid relying on damage that badly underkills or massively overkills the expected target. Both waste list resources.
- AP is critical into tough or armored targets. Include credible high-AP or Deadly threats when the faction has access to them.
- Blast helps into chaff and lower-point games where many small bodies are expected.
- Furious, Shred, Surge, Impact, strong quality, and high attack count are signals that a unit may be a primary damage tool.
- Fearless, banners, morale buffs, and durable Tough profiles matter because units must keep activating and contesting after taking losses.
- If a major enemy unit can be wiped in one activation, it is a credible focus target. If it takes too many activations to kill, advise playing around it or ignoring it.
- Duplicate units that are core to the strategy or highly cost-effective. Avoid doubling fragile, low-value units when the second copy only creates overkill.
- Heroes are best when they multiply a key unit, protect a necessary spell or aura, or fix morale. Expensive heroes need a clear job.
