# Archetypes and Playstyles

- Name an army archetype and playstyle explicitly in advice. The list should have a coherent way to win on the board.
- Offensive armies spend more on damage than durability. They try to out-trade, remove enemy units in one activation, and win with pressure.
- Defensive armies invest in durability, morale, and counter-strike. They aim to absorb the first hit, hold space, and win through staying power.
- Elite armies bring fewer, more reliable, more expensive models. They often use high quality, Fearless, or high Tough values, but can still be aggressive.
- Horde armies maximize wounds and activations. They win by forcing the opponent to answer too many bodies and board-control problems.
- Positional counterplay uses Scout, Ambush, Aircraft, Flying, Fast, or redeployment to win through movement and timing.
- Flank overload commits heavy resources to one side, wins that side decisively, and stalls or trades elsewhere.
- Shove it in applies immediate board pressure and restricts the opponent's movement from the start.
- Slow roll advances gradually, trades at range, preserves resources, and commits late for objectives.
- Round 2 go builds around an explosive second round, often using Ambush, casters, buffs, or other delayed pressure.
- Control lists disrupt the opponent with movement manipulation, difficult terrain, debuffs, mind control, or denial tools.
